Francesinha

What is it ?

Francesinha is a Portuguese sandwich, originally from Porto, made with layers of toasted bread and assorted hot meats such as roast, steak, wet-cured ham, linguiça, or chipolata over which sliced cheese is melted by the ladling of a near-boiling tomato-and-beer sauce called molho de francesinha It is typically served with french fries.

- Wikipedia

Ingredients

Francesinha ingredients

  • 2 Slices of bread.
  • 1 Linguiça.
  • 1 Sausage.
  • 2 Slices of ham.
  • 2 Slices of Baloney.
  • 1 Steak.
  • 5-6 Slices of Flamengo cheese slices.
  • 1 Egg (optional).
  • Fries on the side (optional).

Sauce ingredients

  • 1 Medium yellow onion.
  • 1.tbsp Olive Oil
  • 13.5 tbsp White wine
  • 4 tbsp Ketchup
  • 4 tbsp Tomato puree/paste
  • 2 tbsp Oxtail soup mix
  • 6.5 tbsp Whiskey
  • 3.5 tbsp Tawny Port wine
  • 2x 250 ml Pilsner beer
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp Piripiri sauce
  • 1 uni. Chicken Knorr Natura Bouillion
  • 6.5 tbsp Milk
  • 1 Tsp Flour

Francesinha Recipe - Step by step

  1. Slightly toast the bread – in the oven or in the toaster but without smooshing it, so it doesn’t lose its thickness.
  2. Cook the steak medium/rare and allow it to rest away from the heat for a few minutes, to retain the juices. Slit it slightly so that it’s easier to cut later on.
  3. Cut the linguiça and the Italian sausage length wise and trim them, if necessary, to match the size of the bread. Cook them in the same pan as the steak.
  4. Fry the egg(s) in the same pan again (optional). Fry the potatoes on the side (opcional).
  5. Assembling: after toasted, place one slice of bread in a deep dish that can go in the oven, and lay the slices of ham and baloney over the bread; place the sausage and the linguiça interspersed, side by side, and then the steak; place the second slice of bread on top and cover it all with cheese slices.
  6. Turn on the broil and place it in the oven until the cheese has melted.
  7. Remove from the oven, (place the egg on top), pour at least two ladles of sauce over the francesinha and serve immediatly.
  8. Pour the rest of the sauce in a gravy boat and serve seperately (as well as the fries).

Sauce recipe - Step by step

  1. Dice the onion and braise in the olive oil, until soft and transparent.
  2. Add the wine, the ketchup and the tomato puree/paste – bring it to a boil and allow it to stay there while you prepare the next step.
  3. Disolve the oxtail soup mix in 520 ml of boiling water and add to the pot.
  4. Add the whiskey and the Port, and allow it to boil for 35 minutes until the alcohol evaporates (with the lid half on).
  5. Use the immersion blender to turn the mix into a smooth sauce.
  6. Add the beers, the Worcestershire sauce, the piripiri sauce and the boullion.
  7. Disolve the flour in the milk, add to the pot and allow it to boil for 15 minutes, with the lid on.
  8. Lower the heat to the minimum until it’s time to serve. Resist the temptation to open the pot as much as possible!
