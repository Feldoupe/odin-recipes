Spaghetti Carbonara

What is it?

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. The hot pasta is tossed with the raw eggs, creating a creamy sauce that coats the spaghetti. Pecorino Romano cheese is traditionally used, though Parmesan can also be added. The dish is simple yet flavorful and is a staple of Roman cuisine.

Ingredients

Carbonara ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or guanciale
  • 3 large eggs
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves of garlic (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt

Carbonara Recipe - Step by step

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, fry the pancetta in a large pan until it becomes crispy. Optionally, add the garlic to the pan for additional flavor, then remove it after the pancetta is cooked.
  3. In a bowl, beat the eggs and mix in the grated Pecorino Romano and Parmesan cheeses. Add a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Drain the spaghetti, reserving some of the pasta water. Add the spaghetti to the pan with the pancetta and mix well.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat, and quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture, ensuring that the pasta is well coated. Add a little pasta water if needed to create a smooth, creamy sauce.
  6. Serve immediately, with extra cheese and black pepper if desired.
